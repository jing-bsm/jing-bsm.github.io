Tips for small-medium business websites (Continue)

Tested

Technology changes! A professional website should give you a check list before the website goes live. Here is only a small portion of the list:

  • Marketing Oriented. A website should match the marketing objectives, such as logo, color, layout, and etc. The website is supposed to work with business cards, brochures and other marketing strategies.
  • Usability. A website should be displayed well in the latest two versions of Internet Explorer, Firefox, Opera, and Safari. It may need to browse well in an IPhone, Android and Blackberry as there are more people using mobile devices online. In addition, your business website should be displayed in lower screen resolution to serve low end and early computers, such as netbooks.
  • Accessibility. Website should be easy to browse and each page should be found within five clicks.
  • Email. At least one professional mail is needed, and the email address posted on the website should be encrypted to prevent email spam. One online submission form may also be needed for people leaving messages with public computers.
  • Maintainability. Website should be easy to maintain.
  • And much more.

Healthy

Keep broken links, missing pictures and other unhealthy contents away from your website. As time goes by, your website should monitor those changes. Keeping the website up to date is important.

Overall, websites are treated as the Representatives of your business. Having a “SMOOTH” websites can greatly helping the succeed of small-medium businesses. We hope every business could build up excellent customer and public relationships through the internet.
